About

A Chef Shaped by the World

Five continents, seven countries, three languages — and one lifelong devotion to the table.

Essam Kabil's path to the kitchen didn't start in one. He began with engineering studies at Alexandria University and a degree in commerce from Zagazig University in Egypt — a practical, disciplined foundation that would later show up in how he runs a kitchen and manages an event. But it was a restless curiosity about the world that changed his direction.

A season working in the officers' restaurant of the French Foreign Legion in French Guiana put him, for the first time, in service to a discerning, exacting table — and lit a fascination with France that never went out. That fascination became a formal education: Essam trained at Le C.R.E.T. Culinary School in Briançon, in the French Alps, studying French wines and classical table-side service, including service à la Russe, the elegant art of plating and finishing dishes tableside.

He went on to specialize further in crêpe-making in Vannes, Brittany, before opening his own restaurant, Cleopatra Restaurant & Crêperie, in Montpellier — proof, early on, that he could build something of his own and make it work.

The decades since have taken him through some of the world's most respected hospitality names: four different Ritz-Carlton properties spanning Spain, Egypt, and the U.S., the Oberoi in Saudi Arabia, a luxury cruise line, and executive chef posts from Cairo to the Carolinas. Along the way he became fluent in three languages and comfortable in kitchens that ranged from grand hotel banquets to intimate crêperies — and developed a genuine expertise in vegetarian, vegan, and macrobiotic cooking well before it was fashionable to offer.

Today, Essam brings all of it — the French technique, the Mediterranean and Middle Eastern flavors of home, the discipline of five-star hotel kitchens, and the warmth of a man who has cooked for strangers on every continent and made them feel like guests in his own home — directly to your table, your event, or your kitchen counter.
